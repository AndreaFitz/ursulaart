About the Artist

Biography

Ursula Newell-Walker, though born in Lancashire, grew up in Australia and worked and exhibited there, in Java and Eire, before returning to England. She comes from a family of painters and field naturalists.

She is fascinated by the way in which light, the seasons and the changing moods and internal stances of the viewer imbue the ordinary with something akin to mystery. Her work explores the transcendent within the everyday and is influenced by the 'visionary' tradition; from Samuel Palmer to David Blackburn.

All her works are about place, time and light, and that which lies always at the threshold of consciousness. Each painting is a glimpse of another reality; always there, but often unnoticed.
